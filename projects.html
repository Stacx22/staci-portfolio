Data Science & Analytics Portfolio

Below is a selection of projects completed across my graduate coursework in data science, predictive analytics, and data visualization. Each project includes a brief summary and a link to the corresponding GitHub repository, where you can review code, documentation, and results.

Fertility Outcomes Prediction

Built an R-based supervised learning pipeline using tidymodels to predict altered fertility outcomes from lifestyle and health-related variables. Compared logistic regression, random forest, and XGBoost models, with a focus on interpretability, model evaluation, and ethical use of health data.

Rossmann Store Sales Forecasting

Forecasted daily store sales using historical transaction data, promotions, and calendar features. Implemented regression-based models and tree-based ensembles in Python to support staffing, inventory, and promotional planning decisions, with performance measured using RMSE and MAPE.

View Repository →

Telecom Customer Churn Modeling

Developed a logistic regression churn model to identify high-risk telecom customers using demographic, billing, and service usage features. Focused on interpretable coefficients, confusion matrix analysis, and ROC AUC to support targeted retention strategies that are transparent to business stakeholders.

View Repository →

Apple Quality Prediction

Used the apple_quality.xlsx dataset to predict apple quality from physical and sensory attributes such as sweetness, crunchiness, juiciness, and acidity. Implemented both regression and classification models, with random forest identification of sweetness and crunchiness as key drivers of perceived quality.

Childcare Costs & Socioeconomic Patterns

Analyzed the National Database of Childcare Prices to understand how childcare costs vary by age group, provider type, and geography, and how these trends relate to female unemployment. Delivered insights through an interactive Power BI dashboard and static R-based visuals.

White House Visitor Logs Dashboard

Created a Python-based dashboard to explore patterns in White House visitor access records, focusing on timing, frequency, and location of visits. Applied thoughtful visual design and ethics to support civic transparency without exposing individual-level details.

Kia & Hyundai Theft Visualization

Built an R Markdown report with six visuals to communicate the rise of Kia and Hyundai thefts across U.S. cities. Included pie, donut, stacked bar, treemap, area, and stacked area charts tailored to municipal decision-makers and law enforcement analysts.

Lottery Trends Across U.S. Games

Used Power BI to analyze winning-number patterns for Mega Millions, Powerball, and Pick 10. Created histograms, density plots, scatterplots, small multiples, bubble charts, and donut charts to explore randomness and promote transparency in public lottery systems.

Airline Complaint Analysis

Examined passenger complaint data by airport, category, and subcategory using R. Produced heat maps, treemaps, box plots, spatial maps, bar charts, and time series to help airline and airport leaders pinpoint the most pressing service issues and prioritize improvements.

Autism Data Ethics Exploration

Wrote a research-driven white paper on autism spectrum disorder, examining assumptions, ethical issues in early diagnosis and intervention, and the implications of biomarker research. Emphasized neurodiversity, autonomy, and inclusive research practices.
